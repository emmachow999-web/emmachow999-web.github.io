  • California has an extensive set of privacy laws protecting consumer, online, financial, health, communication, and student information.
  • These laws generally require transparency, limit data sharing, provide individual rights, and impose penalties for misuse.

California Constitution: Guarantees a fundamental right to privacy. Enforced through private lawsuits. SECTION 1. All people are by nature free and independent and have inalienable rights. Among these are enjoying and defending life and liberty, acquiring, possessing, and protecting property, and pursuing and obtaining safety, happiness, and privacy.
